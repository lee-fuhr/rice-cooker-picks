Best rice cookers for 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Our top pick for most households is the Toshiba TRCS01, which delivers reliable fuzzy-logic cooking and a compact footprint at a reasonable price.

If you want maximum features and don’t mind spending more, the Zojirushi NS-TSC10 offers stellar build and texture; the Cuckoo CRP-HS0609W is the editors’ pick for its combined pressure and fuzzy-logic versatility; the Aroma ARC-150SB is the budget champion for large batches; and the Black+Decker RC506 is a simple, affordable runner-up for basic needs.

A good rice cooker can transform a weeknight dinner, freeing up stove space and delivering consistent, fluffy rice without guesswork. Beyond plain white rice, the best models handle brown rice, sushi rice, quinoa, and even porridge or cakes with programmable settings and fuzzy logic that adjusts temperature and time in real time. When choosing, focus on capacity (how many cups you typically cook), cooking logic (microcomputer or fuzzy logic versus simple on/off), and inner-pot material. Stainless steel or thick nonstick coatings improve durability and browning. A delay timer and keep-warm function add convenience. Avoid vague “one-touch” claims; look for specific settings that match the grains you eat most often.
